## Abstract 

We propose a 3D novel sparse-view synthesis framework for unconstrained real-world scenarios that contain distractors. Unlike existing methods that primarily perform novel-view synthesis from a sparse set of constrained images without transient elements or leverage unconstrained dense image collections to enhance 3D representation in real-world scenarios, our method not only effectively tackles sparse unconstrained image collections, but also shows high-quality 3D render-ing results. To do this, we introduce reference-guided view refinement with a diffusion model using a transient mask and a reference image to enhance the 3D representation and mitigate artifacts in rendered views. Furthermore, we address sparse regions in the Gaussian field via pseudo-view generation along with a sparsity-aware Gaussian replication strategy to amplify Gaussians in the sparse regions. Extensive experiments on publicly available datasets demonstrate that our methodology consistently outperforms existing methods ( e.g. , PSNR - 17.2% ↑,SSIM - 10.8% ↑, LPIPS - 4.0% ↑) and provides high-fidelity 3D rendering results. This advancement paves the way for realizing unconstrained real-world scenarios without labor-intensive data acquisition. Our project page is available at here 

## 1 Introduction 

Rendering a realistic representation of a 3D scene from a collection of images is a fundamental challenge in computer vision and graphics. Solving this problem is crucial for applications such as robotics [ 14 , 15 ], autonomous driving [ 11 , 78 , 58 ] VR/AR [ 14 , 49 , 56 ], and 3D content generation [ 26 ,41 , 44 ]. While contemporary methods [ 5, 29 , 6, 60 ] excel at reconstructing 3D scenes from a dense

Prior work. To overcome these challenges, several approaches have been proposed, which gen-erally fall into three paradigms: (i) multi-stage training-based methods [ 69 , 9 , 77 ] that handle unobserved areas by increasing training time with multi training stages; (ii) depth regularization-based techniques [ 23 , 79 , 19 ] that leverage foundation models ( e.g., DepthAnything [ 67 ], DPT [ 40 ]) to regularize depth; (iii) diffusion-based methods [ 63 , 61 , 7, 36 , 70 ] that refine rendered views or generate unobserved viewpoints to improve geometric consistency and distill a 3D representation. 

Challenge. Although prior works have shown that high-quality 3D rendering can be realized from a sparse set of constrained images, these approaches still struggle to utilize unconstrained real-world scenarios due to the presence of distractors, appearance variations, and dynamic objects (Fig. 1). To address this issue, several methods [ 30 , 20 , 55 , 73 , 38 ] tackle distractors via vision foundation models (e.g., SAM [ 18 ], DINO [ 35 ], Diffusion [ 54 ]). Nonetheless, these techniques struggle to identify distractors due to the limited number of images, making it difficult to generalize. 

Motivation. Inspired to solve these challenging issues, we observe that simply leveraging a sparse set of real-world images leads to multi-view inconsistency and struggles to capture distractors due to limited scenes. In particular, we analyze how a sparse set of real-world images can fail to provide high-quality results in 3D novel view synthesis (Fig. 2). Furthermore, we note that simply leveraging positional gradients to mitigate sparsity in the Gaussian field leads to artifact issues in the rendering results. 

Solution. To address this dilemma, we introduce SaveWildGS, a framework that enables 3D novel view synthesis from a sparse set of real-world images with distractors. Specifically, we propose a reference-guided view refinement by utilizing a redesigned diffusion model to refine rendered views using a reference view and a transient mask. Moreover, to tackle sparsity in the Gaussian field we leverage generated pseudo-labels and amplify the Gaussians to construct a dense Gaussian field. We also employ low-rank adaptation (LoRA) and the score distillation sampling (SDS) loss to prevent model collapse and maintain geometric consistency. Extensive experiments on various scenarios ( e.g., NeRF On-the-go [ 43 ], Photo Tourism [ 51 ], LLFF [ 32 ]) demonstrate that SaveWildGS outperforms existing methods. 

Key distinction. To the best of our knowledge, SaveWildGS is the first framework to tackle 3D reconstruction from a sparse set of real-world images that include diverse distractors. This is not an incremental extension of existing methods. Notably, SaveWildGS (i) bridges the gap between a sparse set of images and real-world scenarios with distractors for the first time, (ii) enables high-quality 3D rendering results without sacrificing time complexity, and (iii) can be utilized in unconstrained/constrained scenarios via plug-and-play. In summary, our contributions are as follows. • Impact. We propose a novel framework for sparse-view synthesis in real unconstrained scenarios with miscellaneous distractors, without significantly increasing time complexity. • Versatility. Our method can be utilized through plug-and-play with a redesigned one-step diffusion model in constrained or unconstrained scenarios. • Effectiveness. Extensive results demonstrate that SaveWildGS outperforms the prior methods (e.g., PSNR -17.2% ↑, SSIM - 10.8% ↑, LPIPS - 4.0% ↑) and creates new avenues for sparse scenarios. 

## 2 Related Work 

Dense-view synthesis for unconstrained scenarios. Recently, neural radiance fields (NeRFs) [ 32 ]and 3D Gaussian splatting (3DGS) [ 16 ] have been widely used in 3D reconstruction. Prior works fall 2into three paradigms: (i) residual-based approaches [ 4, 24 , 73 , 38 ] that only leverage photometric error to identify transient elements from training images; (ii) semantic-based methods [ 20 , 45 ,64 ] that utilize semantic features obtained from a foundation model ( e.g., SAM [ 18 ], DINO [ 35 ], Diffusion [ 54 ]) to generate transient masks; (iii) heuristic-based techniques [ 2 , 3 , 53 ] that exploit 2D masks from SAM to construct transient masks. Although existing methods show impressive results, they rely on a dense image collection which necessitates a large amount of time to collect. In contrast, our approach handles a sparse set of images that include distractors for 3D novel view synthesis. 

Sparse-view synthesis. While previous works [ 29 , 5, 6, 60 ] utilize dense image collections with-out transient elements, recent approaches make an effort to achieve 3D reconstruction from a sparse set of images. These techniques can be separated into the following: (i) multi-stage training-based methods [ 69 , 9, 77 ] that render 3D views via diverse training strategies; (ii) depth-based approaches [ 23 , 79 , 19 ] that leverage a foundation model ( e.g., Depth Anything [ 67 ], DPT [ 40 ]) to regularize depth and rendered views; (iii) diffusion-based methods [ 63 , 61 , 7 , 36 , 70 ] that refine rendered views and generate pseudo-views to handle deficient camera viewpoints. Nevertheless, these methodologies are difficult to utilize in real-world scenarios due to distractors. Several works [ 22 , 25 , 74 ] try to address sparse-view synthesis, yet these techniques only focus on appearance variation and not on substantial occlusions. In contrast, our method can be used with sparse views that include distractors, providing high-quality 3D rendering results. 

Adaptive density control. An adaptive density control (ADC) strategy in 3DGS works via two operations: pruning and densification. These actions fill sparse regions and add fine details. Current works [ 76 , 1 , 65 ] aim to enhance the Gaussian fields for various purposes including (i) improving memory efficiency [ 33 , 21 ], (ii) filling in deficient regions [ 63 , 8], and (iii) enhancing rendering quality and consistency [ 76 , 72 ]. Nonetheless, these approaches focus on constrained images, which makes them difficult to utilize in real-world settings. Several methods [ 10 , 38 ] address ADC in unconstrained scenarios to align the Gaussians, yet they are not easy to use with a sparse image collection due to a lack of consideration for sparsity in the Gaussian field. Specifically, ForestSplats [ 38 ] introduced uncertainty primitives to effectively align the Gaussians. Conversely, in this work we amplify the Gaussians to mitigate the sparsity issue and foster consistent 3D rendering quality.

## 4 Method 

Inspired by the preceding observations, we propose a comprehensive framework that leverages 3DGS for novel view synthesis from a sparse set of unconstrained images. As illustrated in Fig. 3, SaveWildGS consists of two key components: (i) reference-guided view refinement, which employs a redesigned DM to refine rendered views by leveraging a reference view and a transient mask (Sec. 4.1); (ii) pseudo-label synthesis to address the issue of sparsity in the Gaussian field via amplification, thus enhancing the 3D rendering quality (Sec. 4.2). Finally, to maintain 3D consistency, we introduce regularization and optimization to mitigate collapse and artifact issues (Sec. 4.3).

4.2 Reference-Guided Pseudo-Label Synthesis Pseudo-label synthesis. Using a sparse set of images presents challenges such as overfitting to the input views and geometric inconsistencies. To mitigate these issues, we utilize a reference view for generating a pseudo-label to distill the 3D representation of the Gaussian field. Nevertheless, simply using the generated pseudo-label ignores the presence of transient elements. Therefore, we create a refined pseudo-label ( ˆIpseudo ) that references a rendered view from other camera perspectives along with a transient mask using Eq. (6) . In particular, we utilize a mask generator to identify regions that need refinement from rendered views in order to generate a refined pseudo-label via Eq. (7) . Lastly, we optimize the Gaussians utilizing the pseudo-label: 

Lpseudo = (1 − λ)L1( ˆI, ˆIpseudo ) + λLD-SSIM ( ˆI, ˆIpseudo ). (9) Additionally, we employ Lpseudo to enhance the 3D representation with Lphoto , attaining multi-view consistency and preventing artifacts and overfitting issues. Note that although we utilize two vision foundation models in the pipeline, the key distinction of our work from prior methods [ 27 , 61 , 19 , 36 ], which also utilize two vision foundation models, is that we efficiently generate a pseudo-label that considers regions that need refinement in scenarios with distractors.

## 5 Experiments 

Datasets, baselines, and metrics. We evaluate SaveWildGS on the NeRF On-the-go [ 43 ] and Photo Tourism [ 51 ] datasets. Following prior approaches [ 20 , 43 , 10 ], we leverage six scenes with distractors and follow the settings of existing methods ( e.g., image resolution, 3D point initialization, test scenes) on the NeRF On-the-go dataset. For the Photo Tourism dataset, we utilize three landmark scenes and abide by the experimental setup of past works [ 20 ]. Furthermore, we also evaluate on the LLFF [ 31 ] dataset to demonstrate the effectiveness of our method in constrained scenarios without distractors by following previous techniques [ 79 , 59 ]. We use COLMAP [ 48 , 47 ] to initialize the point cloud and camera poses. Note that the initialization is estimated from a sparse set of images, resulting in potentially noisy initial conditions. We compare SaveWildGS with the following past work: (i) fundamental methods - 3DGS [ 16 ] and Mip-Splatting [ 71 ]; (ii) few-shot-based techniques -RegNeRF [ 34 ], DiffusionNeRF [ 62 ], FreeNeRF [ 66 ], ReconFusion [ 61 ], FSGS [ 79 ], DropoutGS [ 37 ], and Difix3D+ [ 59 ]; (iii) in-the-wild-based approaches - NeRF-W [ 30 ], Ha-NeRF [ 4], CR-NeRF [ 68 ], WildGaussian [ 20 ], GS-W [ 73 ], RobustSplat [ 10 ], DroneSplat [ 53 ] and SparseGS-W [ 25 ]. Following common practice, we adopt the PSNR, SSIM [57], and LPIPS [75] metrics to assess performance. 

Implementation details. We implemented our method based on RobustSplat. For the NeRF On-the-go and the LLFF datasets, we employed the Adam [ 17 ] optimizer without weight decay and set the total training iterations to 20 K. For the Photo Tourism dataset, we set the total training iterations to 50 K. We introduced Lpseudo in the total loss after 5 K iterations to utilize a reference view. Furthermore, to generate transient masks, we leveraged the frozen Grounded SAM [ 42 ]. Although a one-step DM [ 59 ] is redesigned to suit our setting, we followed the default hyperparameter values. ## 7 Limitations and Conclusion 

Fig. 10: Overlapped camera perspectives represent a potential limitation. 

Limitations. Although SaveWildGS yields state-of-the-art results on 3D novel view synthesis for sparse sets of im-ages, there are two limitations: (i) it can struggle to handle an object that may be static in the whole sequence ( e.g., parked vehicles, standing pedestrians, etc. ), which makes it hard to identify transient elements from the background; (ii) as depicted in Fig. 10, utilizing overlapped camera perspectives may reduce the regions available to reference, leading to a degraded 3D representation. To address these issues, incorporating prior knowledge about transient ele-ments along with an additional inpainting model could be a promising direction for future work. 

Conclusion. In this paper we presented SaveWildGS, a novel framework for 3D reconstruction from sparse images with transient elements. SaveWildGS consists of a simple and effective reference-guided view refinement strategy that mitigates artifacts in rendered views via a reference view and a transient mask. Furthermore, to tackle sparsity in the Gaussian field we introduced reference-guided pseudo-label synthesis, which distills 3D representations using a DM. Our method replicates Gaussians by considering sparse regions to enhance the 3D representation and construct a dense Gaussian field. Extensive experimental results on constrained/unconstrained scenarios demonstrate that SaveWildGS achieves significant qualitative and quantitative improvements over the state of the art. This advancement opens new avenues for 3D novel-view synthesis from a sparse set of real-world images. 9References

## A Related Work 

Sparse-view synthesis for unconstrained scenarios. Several other works [ 74 , 25 ] address sparse-view synthesis in real-world scenarios. However, these methods only focus on appearance variation settings, not on distractors. Compared to diffusion-based techniques [ 19 , 36 , 59 ], SaveWildGS considers distractors to refine rendered views using a transient mask and a reference image without penalizing time efficiency. 

Feed-forward methods. Other works generate new Gaussians utilizing pretrained transformers [ 13 ,28 ]. Although these methods show impressive results in constrained scenarios, SaveWildGS differs as we focus on unconstrained real-world scenes that include distractors. 

## B Implementation Details 

All optimization and training were performed on a single NVIDIA A40 GPU. For our choice of learning rate, we followed RobustSplat [ 10 ]. To train LoRA, we set the learning rate to 1e-4 across all datasets. For the DM, we redesigned Difix3D+ [ 59 ] to utilize a reference image and a transient mask. Rendered views were refined by randomly selecting a reference view from diverse camera perspectives. We leveraged Grounded SAM [ 42 ] to construct transient masks by following the default settings. To obtain transient masks from Grounded SAM, we used text descriptions Ttext such as humans, robots, and vehicles to capture distractors across all the datasets. For SAGR, we generated new Gaussians by following recent methods [ 10 , 38 ] that consider sparse regions in the 3D Gaussian field. Furthermore, we also employed the original Lphoto (2), with a redefined Lphoto , to train a 3D Gaussian field. To select training views from dense image collections, we randomly sampled training views by following prior approaches [ 59 , 79 ]. Our approach is shown in Algorithm 1. 

Architecture details. We redesigned a one-step DM based on Difix3D+ [ 59 ]. We followed the default setting ( e.g., guidance, noise level). However, for cross-attention we utilized a key ℓK and value 

ℓV obtained from a reference image. For self-attention, we use the key ℓK and value ℓV obtained from a rendered image. Furthermore, we randomly sampled reference images from diverse camera perspectives. Although the images may be corrupted, SaveWildGS can effectively refine them via its mask generator. 

Baseline details. To ensure a fair comparison with existing methods, we reproduced our results using publicly available source code. Although several methods [ 19 , 36 , 61 ] are related to our work, we excluded them if their source code was not available or no comparable evaluation metrics were reported.

## E Limitations and Future Work 

Limitations. Although SaveWildGS shows impressive results, it has two limitations. First, despite the DM refining corrupted images, the refined images may contain a slight amount of noise as highlighted in Fig. 14. Second, although the mask generator is good at capturing noise and blur in the rendered images, it can miss other artifacts. To address this problem, a super-resolution model can be used to refine corrupted regions. 

Future Work. SaveWildGS provides high-quality rendering results in constrained and unconstrained real-world scenarios. Nonetheless, since our framework utilizes a Gaussian field, it may struggle to bridge the gap between physical AI and real-world scenarios due to the presence of artifacts. We hope to close this gap by exploring mesh-based techniques in the future.
