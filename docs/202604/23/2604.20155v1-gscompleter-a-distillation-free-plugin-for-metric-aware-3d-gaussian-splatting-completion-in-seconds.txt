## Abstract 

While 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has revo-lutionized real-time rendering, its performance degrades significantly under sparse-view extrapo-lation, manifesting as severe geometric voids and artifacts. Existing solutions primarily rely on an iterative “Repair-then-Distill” paradigm, which is inherently unstable and prone to overfitting. In this work, we propose GSCompleter, a distillation-free plugin that shifts scene completion to a sta-ble “Generate-then-Register” workflow. Our ap-proach first synthesizes plausible 2D reference images and explicitly lifts them into metric-scale 3D primitives via a robust Stereo-Anchor mech-anism. These primitives are then seamlessly in-tegrated into the global context through a novel Ray-Constrained Registration strategy. This shift to a rapid registration paradigm delivers superior 3DGS completion performance across three dis-tinct benchmarks, enhancing the quality and ef-ficiency of various baselines and achieving new SOTA results.

This breakthrough inspires a pivotal question: Can we leverage these metric priors to eliminate expensive scale optimization, transforming scene completion into a rapid registration task? 

In this paper, we rethink the unstable “Repair-then-Distill” optimization, advocating a paradigm shift toward a robust “Generate-then-Register” workflow. We introduce GSCom-pleter , a novel plugin for 3DGS scenes completion. Our core insight is that Gaussian primitives with accurate met-ric scale enable rapid registration, eliminating the need for expensive alignment. We first employ a 3D-aware gen-erative prior (PE-Field) (Bai et al., 2025) to synthesize a plausible image in the novel view. Rather than iteratively distilling this content via unstable optimization, we use a feed-forward Gaussian regressor to lift the 2D image into 3D Gaussian primitives with precise physical scale. Capi-talizing on this metric scale advantage, we transform scene completion into a rapid registration task. This paradigm shift effectively bypasses the instability of iterative densifi-cation and achieves superior performance in both rendering fidelity and inference speed. Results on multiple benchmarks across three categories of baselines (feed-forward, distillation-based, and registration-based) demonstrate that integrating GSCompleter not only consistently boosts PSNR by up to 2.41 dB , but also achieves superior stability over distillation-based methods such as 3DGS with densification ( 19.39 dB vs. 16.69 dB). Most notably, it accelerates inference by orders of magni-tude ( seconds vs. minutes) compared to registration-based approaches like RegGS while delivering significantly higher fidelity ( 29.27 dB vs. 24.65 dB). Overall, our contributions are summarized as follows: • We propose a novel “Generate-then-Register” paradigm that achieves high-fidelity completion in seconds, effectively eliminating the geometric artifacts inherent to iterative densification. • We design a Stereo-Anchor Selection mechanism that identifies optimal reference views to secure robust met-ric depth, thereby enhancing registration precision. • We introduce a novel ray-constrained strategy to re-strict geometric updates along camera rays, enabling fast and precise registration without texture drifting. • Extensive evaluations confirm that GSCompleter sig-nificantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in both rendering fidelity and efficiency, highlighting its signif-icant potential for 3DGS applications. 

## 2. Related Work 

Feed-forward 3D Reconstruction. 

NeRF (Mildenhall et al., 2021) and 3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023) set the standard for view synthesis, with subsequent works attempting various improvements to enhance recon-struction quality (Niemeyer et al., 2022; Zhu et al., 2024; Paliwal et al., 2024). However, these methods suffer from the prohibitive latency of per-scene optimization, which fundamentally precludes their application in real-time or scalable scenarios. Consequently, research has pivoted toward generalizable feed-forward models for single-pass inference (Jiang et al., 2025). Early generalized fields (Yu et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2021) and MVS-guided methods (Chen et al., 2021) faced rendering bottlenecks, while explicit grids (Fridovich-Keil et al., 2022; 2023) encountered memory constraints. Recently, feed-forward Gaussian regression has emerged as the efficient paradigm for scene reconstruction. Pixel-aligned architectures like PixelSplat (Charatan et al., 2024), TranSplat (Zhang et al., 2025), and MVSplat (Chen et al., 2024) have established strong baselines, with subsequent works further integrating depth priors (Xu et al., 2025). Most recently, VolSplat (Wang et al., 2025) has shifted this paradigm by introducing a voxel-aligned framework. How-ever, these models fail to hallucinate unobserved content, leading to geometric collapse in occluded areas. 

Generative 3D Reconstruction. Diffusion-based mod-els (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022) have signifi-cantly advanced NVS by introducing robust priors for in-painting and completion (Tang et al., 2024). Leveraging camera-conditioned generation (Liu et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2025b; Gao et al., 2024; Sargent et al., 2024), the current state-of-the-art largely follows a “render-repair-reoptimize” workflow. Prominent examples like 3DGS-Enhancer (Liu et al., 2024) and DiFix3D+ (Wu et al., 2025a) utilize dif-fusion to rectify rendering artifacts, subsequently distilling these corrections back into the 3D representation. While promising, such iterative optimization is inherently slow and susceptible to geometric drift. Our approach, GSCompleter, circumvents these issues by casting the generative prior as a rigid geometric entity, allowing for explicit integration via registration rather than implicit, unstable distillation. 

Gaussian Registration. While traditional point cloud registration (Besl & McKay, 1992; Pan et al., 2018; Qin et al., 2023) and NeRF-based approaches (Goli et al., 2023; Hausler et al., 2024) attempt to align local regions for global mapping, they often suffer from limited generalization or low computational efficiency. ## 3. Methodology 

3.1. Preliminaries 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) We represent the scene us-ing 3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023), a set of explicit, differentiable primitives. Each Gaussian is parameterized by its mean po-sition μ ∈ R3, covariance matrix Σ, opacity α ∈ [0 , 1] , and spherical harmonic (SH) coefficients c. To ensure positive semi-definiteness, the covariance Σ is decomposed into a scaling vector s ∈ R3 and a rotation quaternion q ∈ R4,derived as Σ = RSS ⊤R⊤, where S = diag (s) and R is the rotation matrix from q.

Positional-Encoding Field (PE-Field) We leverage the PE-Field (Bai et al., 2025) to enable geometry-aware view synthesis. Unlike standard DiTs that operate on a 2D plane, this framework extends positional encodings into a struc-tured 3D volumetric field. Specifically, it embeds image tokens into 3D space by encoding their dense coordinates 

(x, y, z ) into the attention mechanism via 3D-aware Rotary Positional Encodings (RoPE). By explicitly injecting depth information, this formulation forces the generative model to reason about the underlying 3D scene manifold, ensuring strict perspective consistency across views. 

3.2. Overview 

We propose GSCompleter , a novel plugin that shifts 3DGS completion task from the unstable “Repair-then-Distill” op-timization to a robust and rapid “Generate-then-Register” paradigm. Given a context scene Gctx reconstructed from sparse ob-servations Iobs , our goal is to seamlessly integrate missing geometry from a target viewpoint Pt. As illustrated in Fig. 2, the proposed pipeline proceeds in four stages: 3GSCompleter: A Distillation-Free Plugin for Metric-Aware 3D Gaussian Splatting Completion in Seconds

By leveraging stereo matching priors to establish an absolute metric scale, this initialization effectively reduces the com-pletion problem to a direct registration task.

We address this via a coarse-to-fine registration strategy: we first employ RANSAC to secure a robust global alignment, followed by a novel 1-DoF Ray-Space Optimization . ## 4. Experimental Setup 

Datasets and Evaluation Protocol. We evaluate GSCom-pleter on RealEstate10K (Zhou et al., 2018), ACID (Liu et al., 2021), and DL3DV (Ling et al., 2024). Unlike base-lines that primarily focus on view interpolation, we design a 2-view extrapolation setting to validate the model’s ability to synthesize large-scale unobserved regions. We define this evaluation protocol as an n-k configuration, where two context views (I1

> ctx

, I 2

> ctx

) are selected from a sequence of k frames to synthesize a third unobserved tar-get view Itgt . Specifically, we employ an n-30 setting for RealEstate10K and ACID, and an n-10 setting for DL3DV to account for its motion complexity. This wide-baseline setup requires the model to actively complete missing ge-ometry and textures, rather than merely aggregating infor-mation from overlapping regions. (See Appendix A.1 for details) 

Metrics. We employ PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS to evaluate novel view quality. To evaluate geometric consistency, we report AbsRel, Chamfer Distance (CD), and F-Score. These metrics quantify the alignment error between the generated target primitives and the context primitives. 

Baselines. We evaluate GSCompleter by adding it to state-of-the-art models: MVSplat (Chen et al., 2024), Depth-Splat (Xu et al., 2025), and VolSplat (Wang et al., 2025). As a plugin, GSCompleter is integrated into these base-lines (denoted as Method+Ours) to show performance gains. Our evaluation primarily focuses on pixel-aligned meth-ods (MVSplat and DepthSplat). To demonstrate generality, we also extend to the voxel-aligned formulation (VolSplat). Additionally, we compare our approach against 3DGS den-sification and the registration-based baseline RegGS (Cheng et al., 2025). 

Implementation Details. GSCompleter is implemented in PyTorch and all experiments are conducted on a single NVIDIA H200 GPU. Following DepthSplat (Xu et al., 2025), we set resolutions to 256 × 256 for RealEstate10K/ACID and 256 × 448 for DL3DV. During inference, we perform 50 iterations of Ray-Constrained Reg-istration ( lr = 0 .01 ), followed by 30 iterations of Opacity-Only Refinement ( lr = 0 .08 ). For the generative prior, we employ only 4 inference steps for the PE-Field, which is suf-ficient to achieve high-fidelity content. All baseline models maintain frozen pre-trained weights throughout the entire process.

Quantitative Results. Tables 1, 2, and 3 summarize the performance across three benchmarks. GSCompleter consis-tently outperforms all baselines, achieving state-of-the-art results. ## 6. Limitations 

Dependency on Depth Accuracy. Our approach relies on accurate metric depth. Baselines with inaccurate depth estimation ( e.g. , PixelSplat (Charatan et al., 2024)) lead to misplaced primitives during registration, thereby degrading completion performance. 

Voxel-Ray Mismatch. While optimal for pixel-aligned methods, our ray constraints conflict with voxel-based struc-tures (e.g., VolSplat (Wang et al., 2025)). This structural mismatch introduces minor boundary artifacts, leading to slight LPIPS degradation despite significant PSNR gains. 

## 7. Conclusion 

In this work, we introduce GSCompleter , a novel “Generate-then-Register” paradigm for 3DGS completion. By leveraging a Stereo-Anchor Selection mechanism, we 8GSCompleter: A Distillation-Free Plugin for Metric-Aware 3D Gaussian Splatting Completion in Seconds 

ensure precise metric depth estimation. Furthermore, we employ a novel Ray-Constrained Registration strategy for rapid alignment, which effectively prevents texture drift and maintains global geometric consistency. Extensive experi-ments demonstrate that GSCompleter achieves significant superiority in 3D Gaussian Splatting scene completion.
